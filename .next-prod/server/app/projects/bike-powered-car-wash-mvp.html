Bike-Powered Car Wash — MVP Build

Reimagining car wash logistics using bike-based micro-operations

Overview

Built an early-stage MVP to test whether a bike-powered fulfillment model could deliver on-demand car wash services with better cost control and faster local coverage.

Problem

Traditional car wash operations rely on heavy field logistics, inconsistent scheduling, and weak visibility on capacity planning. The model is hard to scale while maintaining healthy unit economics.

Solution

Designed a lightweight model around bike-powered micro-operations, zone-based dispatching, and app-to-dashboard coordination for truly on-demand execution.

From heavy operations → to lightweight bike-powered logistics

What I Built

Customer mobile journey: service selection, date/time slot booking, location capture, and payment choice

Operational dashboard: bookings, users/permissions, services, bikers, availability calendar, and zones

Micro-ops control loop: assign riders, monitor throughput, and quickly adjust slot capacity by area

Key Learnings

Small operational primitives compound fast when designed for repeatability.

The fastest path to product clarity is pairing user flow with operator constraints from day one.

Local logistics quality improves when routing, staffing, and availability are managed as one system.

Outcome

The MVP validated a practical path to on-demand service with lower operational weight, stronger execution visibility, and a scalable direction for healthier unit economics.
